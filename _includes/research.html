Research

My current research programme focuses on practice-oriented provable security of symmetric-key cryptography against both classical and post-quantum adversaries. I am particularly interested in efficient constructions, security models for evolving real-world threats, and statistical and combinatorial tools for obtaining tight security guarantees under minimal assumptions.
More broadly, I am also interested in research questions arising from the interplay between cryptology and its allied fields of cybersecurity, combinatorics, statistics, and complexity theory.
